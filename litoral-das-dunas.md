# Archetype: `zona-da-mata-canavieira`

*Origin: Pernambuco pass. Cross-border tag — gazetteer nodes reference this file; see Instances below.*

## Archetype 2: `zona-da-mata-canavieira` (instance: Zona da Mata Pernambucana)

**Identity:** The sugarcane belt — rolling green hills of cane, remnant Atlantic forest patches, and the engenho (colonial sugar mill) as the historical anchor. Reusable along the entire Northeastern coast strip (AL, PB, SE).

**Visual kit:**
- Terrain: soft rolling hills, ribbons of cane fields in different growth stages (a natural patchwork texture from above), dark-green forest remnants in valleys
- Architecture: casa-grande manor + chapel + mill chimney clusters; small towns of single-story colorful rowhouses around a church square; modern usinas (industrial mills) with smokestacks
- Palette: layered greens (cane lime-green vs. forest deep-green), rust-red brick chimneys, white chapels, earth-red dirt roads
- Bird's-eye signature: the cane-field patchwork itself — no other Brazilian landscape looks like it from above

**Soundscape/culture:** maracatu rural, cavalo-marinho folk theater, ciranda; heavy history of slavery and land struggle — treat engenhos as sites of memory, not nostalgia

**Economy hooks:** agriculture cycles (harvest season events), forest gathering, small-town market days

**Landmark templates:**
1. Historic engenho complex (manor, chapel, ruined mill) as a quest/memory site
2. Cane-field crossroads town with church square
3. Atlantic forest reserve fragment with waterfall

**Image prompt template:** *"Top-down view of rolling hills covered in a patchwork of sugarcane fields in varied greens, a colonial sugar mill complex with white manor house, small chapel and tall brick chimney, dark rainforest fragment in a valley, red dirt roads. Isometric game asset, lush and humid atmosphere."*

**Sensitivity note:** This landscape is inseparable from the history of enslaved labor. Archetype NPC culture and quest hooks should center descendant communities (quilombos exist throughout the Mata) with dignity.
