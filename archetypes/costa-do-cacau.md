*Origin: Bahia pass. Cross-border tag — gazetteer nodes reference this file; see Instances below.*

## Archetype 2: `costa-do-cacau` (instance: Litoral Sul)

**Identity:** The cacao coast — and the best "updated framing" case in Brazil. The Jorge Amado-era cacao aristocracy collapsed when the witches' broom fungus devastated plantations in the 1990s. The modern identity is the recovery: bean-to-bar premium chocolate, and cabruca — cacao grown in the shade of native Atlantic forest, an agroforestry system that makes this region a biodiversity showcase rather than a monoculture.

**Visual kit:**
- Terrain: lush coastal hills where cacao groves hide UNDER forest canopy (from above: layered green with only hints of cultivation — the opposite of the Zona da Mata's open cane patchwork), river ports, long beaches
- Architecture: faded cacao-boom mansions and commercial streets (Ilhéus pattern); chocolate ateliers in restored buildings; fishing villages; beach town growth
- Palette: deep layered forest greens, chocolate browns, cream boom-era facades, coastal turquoise
- Bird's-eye signature: canopy-covered hills running straight into beaches — cultivation invisible, forest continuous

**Culture (present tense):** Jorge Amado literary heritage as tourism layer; chocolate festivals; surf culture (Itacaré); strong environmental movement around Mata Atlântica corridors

**Economy hooks:** cacao harvest under canopy (gathering-style loop, distinct from open-field farming), bean-to-bar production chain (farm → fermentation → atelier — great crafting chain), eco/surf tourism

**Landmark templates:**
1. Cabruca grove trail — playable agroforest where crop and wild forest interleave
2. Boom-era mansion converted to chocolate atelier/museum
3. River-mouth port town with surf beach

**Image prompt template:** *"Bird's-eye view of lush forested hills meeting a tropical beach, cacao trees visible beneath taller native canopy, a small river port town with faded elegant early-1900s buildings and colorful fishing boats, continuous deep green landscape, humid bright light. Isometric game asset."*

**Signature-landmark candidates:** Ilhéus (Amado heritage + chocolate), Itacaré (surf/eco).
