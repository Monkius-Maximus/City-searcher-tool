# Archetype: `agreste-feirante`

*Origin: Pernambuco pass. Cross-border tag — gazetteer nodes reference this file; see Instances below.*

## Archetype 3: `agreste-feirante` (instance: Agreste Pernambucano)

**Identity:** The transition belt between coast and drylands — market-town culture, massive open-air feiras, artisan craft, textile industry, and the biggest São João (June festival) tradition in Brazil. Reusable across the Agreste/Borborema strip (PB, RN, AL).

**Visual kit:**
- Terrain: undulating semi-green highlands with granite boulder outcrops (lajedos); scattered green brejos (lush microclimate pockets) contrasting dry surroundings
- Architecture: dense low-rise commercial towns; enormous street market grids of canvas stalls; textile workshop sheds; festival infrastructure (arraial squares with quadrilha dance floors)
- Palette: ochre and terracotta earth, whitewash + vivid commercial facades, granite gray, explosion of festival color (bandeirinhas) in June
- Bird's-eye signature: the feira — hundreds of stall canopies forming a temporary city inside the city, appearing and vanishing by weekday

**Soundscape/culture:** forró in all variants, São João festivals, cordel literature, clay figurine craft (Mestre Vitalino tradition)

**Economy hooks:** THE trading hub of the region — weekly market cycles, textile production chains, craft economy; strongest commerce-gameplay archetype in the state

**Landmark templates:**
1. Mega-feira district (stall grid that transforms by day of week — great systemic mechanic)
2. Artisan hill quarter of clay-figure workshops (Alto do Moura pattern)
3. Granite outcrop viewpoint over the town

**Image prompt template:** *"Bird's-eye view of a bustling market town in semi-arid highlands, huge grid of colorful canvas market stalls filling the central streets, low whitewashed and brightly painted buildings, granite boulder hills in background, strings of festival flags, dry ochre earth. Isometric game asset, dense and lively."*

**"Generic + signature landmark" candidates:** Caruaru (Feira + Alto do Moura — identity too strong for pure generic; give it the feira mega-landmark), Toritama/Santa Cruz do Capibaribe (textile-pole flavor).
