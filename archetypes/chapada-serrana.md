# Archetype: `chapada-serrana`

*Origin: Bahia pass. Cross-border tag — gazetteer nodes reference this file; see Instances below.*

## Archetype 4: `chapada-serrana` (instance: Chapada Diamantina)

**Identity:** The highland heart of Bahia — 19th-century diamond-rush towns fully converted to one of Brazil's premier ecotourism and trekking destinations. The modern economy IS the landscape: national park, guides, pousadas.

**Visual kit:**
- Terrain: flat-topped mesas (tepui-like), canyons, caves with blue pools, high waterfalls (Cachoeira da Fumaça pattern: water turning to mist before reaching ground), high-altitude grasslands with bromeliads
- Architecture: cobbled garimpo-era towns (Lençóis pattern) restored as pousada/guide hubs; trailheads, not roads
- Palette: mesa gray-orange rock, highland grass gold-green, waterfall white, cave-pool unreal blue, town pastels
- Bird's-eye signature: mesas — massive flat geometric rock forms unlike anything else on Brazil's map

**Culture (present tense):** trekking-guide culture (many guides descend from garimpeiro families — a one-generation economy conversion, great NPC backstory material), festival de inverno, quilombola communities in the valleys

**Economy hooks:** expedition prep loop (gear, guides, supplies in town → multi-day trail content), cave/canyon exploration, foraging (sempre-vivas flowers — now sustainably managed after near-extinction from over-harvesting; nice conservation mechanic)

**Landmark templates:**
1. Cobbled ex-mining town as expedition hub
2. Mesa summit with panoramic map-reveal (the Corcovado mechanic, nature edition — Morro do Pai Inácio pattern)
3. Cave with translucent blue pool (timed light-beam event, as in real Poço Azul)

**Image prompt template:** *"Bird's-eye view of a vast highland landscape with flat-topped orange-gray rock mesas, deep green valleys, a thin tall waterfall dissolving into mist, a small cobblestone town with pastel houses nestled at the foot of the range, golden grassland. Isometric game asset, epic scale, crisp light."*

**Signature-landmark candidates:** Lençóis, Morro do Pai Inácio.

## REUSED with variant: `chapada-serrana` → variant `serra-umida` (instance: Serras Úmidas / Baturité)

Cloud-capped highland oases in the dry zone — Serra de Baturité (Guaramiranga) pattern: shade-grown coffee, cool-climate pousada towns, cloud forest remnants. A thin variant on the chapada highland kit (smaller scale, wetter, coffee instead of trekking as the economic anchor). Also retroactively explains Triunfo (PE), which the Pernambuco pass flagged as an oddity.
